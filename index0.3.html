NASDAQ was founded on 8 February 1971 as the world's first electronic stock market. Today it is the second-largest exchange by market cap globally. Its ~3,900 listed companies are disproportionately associated with technology, biotechnology, and high-growth companies.

Why NASDAQ matters: In 2026, as AI transforms industries globally, NASDAQ's performance is essentially a proxy for whether the AI revolution is generating real economic value.

02
Historical Context
Five Decades of Market History
1971
Milestone
NASDAQ launches as the world's first electronic stock market
500 companies on day one. No physical floor — entirely automated through networked market makers.
2000–02
Crisis
Dot-com bust — NASDAQ falls 78%
Crashed from 5,048 to 1,114. Over $5 trillion erased. Took 15 years to fully recover.
2023–25
Milestone
AI supercycle bull market
NASDAQ surges 43% in 2023, 29% in 2024, 21% in 2025 — historic three-year run driven by AI infrastructure spending.
2026
Current
Correction amid geopolitical and valuation pressures
NASDAQ enters correction territory (−8% YTD). US-Iran war, tariff escalation, stretched AI valuations. Moody's recession model at 49%.
